## 1. UNCDF (UN Capital Development Fund)

### The Malawi Fintech Challenge

**Status:** Active program (launched 2024, ongoing)
**Funding:** Up to US$100,000 per grant
**Partners:** Reserve Bank of Malawi (RBM), European Union (EU), Organisation of African, Caribbean and Pacific States (OACPS)

The Malawi Fintech Challenge is UNCDF's flagship fintech initiative in Malawi, operating under the DFS4Resilience (Digital Financial Services for Resilience) programme. It aims to catalyze innovative digital financial solutions to expand access to financial services for underserved communities, particularly smallholder farmers, women, youth, and vulnerable groups.

**Specific Grant Windows (Known):**

| Partner | Grant Amount | Focus |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Telekom Networks Malawi (TNM) | Up to US$100,000 | Digital scorecard/scoring engine using VSLA data |
| Food and Agribusiness Development Company (FADC) | Up to US$100,000 | Digital solution leveraging smallholder farmer data |

**Key Requirements:**
- Selected applicants must co-fund at least 50% of total project cost (monetary or in-kind)
- Targets fintechs, technology companies, payment service providers, and financial technology service providers (both local and international)
- Must have market-ready solutions addressing specified problem statements

**Application Portal:** https://apply.uncdf.org/

**How to Access:**
- Monitor the UNCDF Plug-and-Play e-investment platform for new challenge windows
- The challenge runs in rounds; past rounds had deadlines in March 2024
- Watch for new rounds announced on UNCDF Malawi pages

### DFS4Resilience Programme

**Status:** Active
**Scope:** Leverages digital financial services to enhance resilience of the Malawian economy
**Backed by:** EU and OACPS
**Activities include:**
- DFS Working Group formation (since 2022) focused on networking, collaboration, lobbying, advocacy, training
- Policy dialogue on fintech innovation for financial inclusion
- Capacity building for fintech ecosystem participants

**Sources:**

## 2. FSD Africa (Financial Sector Deepening)

### FSD Malawi Status

**Key Finding:** There is no standalone "FSD Malawi" country entity yet. FSD Africa operates regionally and has indicated plans to expand to Malawi. The FSD Network currently includes dedicated country FSDs in Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda, Zambia, South Africa, Nigeria, and Mozambique.

**However, FSD Africa does fund programs IN Malawi through regional initiatives:**

### Women's World Banking Partnership
- **Amount:** Up to US$7.1 million (5-year program across Nigeria, Malawi, Tanzania)
- **Focus:** Financial services tailored to women (innovative savings products, rural credit)
- **Funder:** UK DFID (now FCDO) through FSD Africa
- **Note:** Malawi is one of three focus countries

### FSD Network Funding Sources
The broader FSD Network attracts funding from:
- UK FCDO (formerly DFID) -- largest funder
- Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency (Sida)
- Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
- Mastercard Foundation
- USAID

**Implication for Malawian businesses:** While there is no dedicated FSD Malawi to apply to directly, FSD Africa's regional programs do target Malawi. Monitor FSD Africa's calls for proposals.

**Sources:**
- https://fsdafrica.org/
- https://fsdnetwork.org/
- https://www.womensworldbanking.org/insights/fsd-africa-womens-world-banking-announce-multi-year-partnership-promote-womens-financial-inclusion-africa/

## 3. World Bank Digital Malawi Projects

### Digital Malawi Acceleration Project (DMAP)

**Status:** Active (approved ~2024-2025)
**Total Funding:** US$150 million (IDA grant)
- Initial tranche: US$70 million from IDA
- Expected increase to US$150 million total
- Predicted to mobilize additional US$50 million in private sector financing
**Implementing Agencies:** Public Private Partnership Commission (PPPC) and Ministry of Information and Digitalization (MID)

**Components relevant to fintech/digital finance:**
1. **Interoperable Data Platforms** -- digital identifications and data exchange platform for government and financial institutions
2. **Digital Payments** -- support for digital payments infrastructure, secure digital authentications for public services
3. **Data Governance** -- policies for data governance and interoperability
4. **Digital Skills Training** -- 10,000 Malawians (50% women)
5. **Internet Access** -- Enhanced access reaching at least 7.5 million people

**How businesses can access this:**
- DMAP will create procurement opportunities for technology vendors, digital payments providers, and platform developers
- Monitor procurement notices at the PPPC and World Bank procurement portals
- Digital skills training contracts will be tendered
- The data exchange platform will need technology partners

### Digital Malawi Foundations Project (Phase 1 -- Completed)

**Funding:** US$72.4 million (IDA)
**Period:** 2017-2024
**Results:** 8.5 million citizens benefited from improved internet access; half were new internet users
**Key Achievement:** Malawi Financial Sector Technical Assistance Project increased adults using financial institutions from 19% (2011) to 40% (2018)

### Inclusive Digitalization in Eastern and Southern Africa Program

**Status:** Active
**Note:** Malawi is a beneficiary country in this regional World Bank program

## 5. Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

### Financial Inclusion Work in Malawi

**Status:** Active (indirect, through intermediaries)
**Direct Grants to Malawi:** Not typically -- the Foundation works through intermediaries

**Programs with Malawi components:**

#### Mojaloop / COMESA Digital Payments
- Foundation funded proof-of-concept in the **Malawi-Zambia trade corridor**
- COMESA is piloting a Mojaloop-backed Inclusive Instant Payment System (IIPS) for SMEs in eight member states **including Malawi**
- Enables instant cross-currency transactions between sellers and buyers along borders
- Open-source software -- any country or entity can adopt it

#### Inclusive Financial Systems Program
- Works to expand access to digital financial services in the world's poorest countries
- Priorities: digital finance infrastructure development, regulatory systems for digital platforms, inclusion and empowerment of women
- Funds "digital public goods" (free, open-source, interoperable)

#### EIB Partnership (2024)
- Gates Foundation and European Investment Bank launched pilot in Africa to improve women entrepreneurs' access to capital

#### FSD Africa Funding
- Gates Foundation is a significant funder of FSD Africa, which operates programs in Malawi

**How to access:**
- The Gates Foundation does **not** accept unsolicited grant applications
- Pathway is through intermediaries: UNCDF, FSD Africa, COMESA, Alliance for Financial Inclusion
- Malawian fintechs should build on Mojaloop open-source infrastructure for potential visibility

**Sources:**

## 9. Reserve Bank of Malawi (RBM)

### Regulatory Framework for Fintech

**Key Finding:** The RBM does **not** currently operate a formal regulatory sandbox in the style of Kenya's CMA or South Africa's FSCA. However, the regulatory environment is evolving rapidly.

#### Digital Banking Directive (2025)
- **New:** Financial Services (Licensing, Regulation and Supervision of Digital Bank) Directive 2025
- Provides framework for digital-only banks to operate in Malawi
- Covers governance, risk management, cybersecurity, data privacy
- **Implication:** Digital banks are now officially on the regulatory roadmap

#### National Financial Inclusion Strategy III (2024-2028)
- **Target:** Increase access to formal financial services to 95% of adults by 2028
- **Current:** 74% formal access (2023 FinScope), 88% formal + informal
- **Priorities:** Deepen access to credit, digital payments, formal services for women, youth, smallholders, MSMEs
- **Strategic Approach:** Create strong and innovative financial service providers; enhance legal and regulatory environment

#### Electronic Payments Regulation
- RBM + Ministry of Trade: mandatory for all licensed businesses to deploy and use electronic payment channels
- Creating regulatory push for digital payments adoption

#### DFS Working Group
- Formed 2022 with UNCDF support
- Focused on networking, collaboration, lobbying, advocacy, training
- Addresses fragmented approaches in DFS ecosystem

#### RBM's Role in Fintech Challenge
- RBM is a **co-lead** of the UNCDF Malawi Fintech Challenge
- Signals openness to innovation and fintech engagement

**What this means for businesses:**
- No sandbox to "apply to" yet, but RBM is receptive to fintech innovation
- Digital bank licensing is now possible under the 2025 Directive
- Engage directly with RBM's Financial Sector Regulation division
- Participate in DFS Working Group activities

**Sources:**
- https://www.rbm.mw/Supervision/FinancialSectorRegulation/
- https://www.afi-global.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/National-Strategy-for-Financial-Inclusion-III.pdf
- https://www.afi-global.org/newsroom/blogs/scaling-up-the-use-of-electronic-payments-in-malawi/

## 10. Accelerators and Foundations

### Tony Elumelu Foundation (TEF)

**Status:** Active, open to all 54 African countries including Malawi
**Grant:** US$5,000 seed capital (non-refundable) per selected entrepreneur
**Program:** 12-week business management training + 9,000+ mentor network
**Total Deployed:** Over US$100 million to 24,000+ entrepreneurs since 2015
**Impact:** 1.5 million direct and indirect jobs created

**Malawi-specific:**
- IYBA-WE4A Entrepreneurship Programme (with EU and GIZ) -- **Malawi is eligible**
- Exclusively for women entrepreneurs with green business ideas

**Application:** Annual cycle -- monitor https://www.tonyelumelufoundation.org/

### Catalyst Fund (BFA Global)

**Status:** Transitioned from grant accelerator to equity VC (2023)
**Historical Model (2015-2022):** Grants and recoverable grants + venture building for inclusive fintech startups
**Current Model:** Equity investment, US$40 million target fund
**Focus shift:** Climate-tech (fintech for climate resilience, sustainable livelihoods, climate-smart essential services)
**Legacy Impact:** Supported 61 startups across 15 emerging markets, reaching 40 million+ underserved customers
**Backed by:** Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation (initially), JP Morgan, UK Aid

**Malawi relevance:** Limited -- Catalyst Fund's equity model now focuses on larger markets. No specific Malawi presence found.

**Sources:**
- https://www.thecatalystfund.com/
- https://www.thecatalystfund.com/inclusive-fintech

### Village Capital

**Status:** Active in Malawi
**Programs in Malawi:**
- **Empowering Sustainable Entrepreneurship in Africa** -- covers Ghana, Kenya, **Malawi**, Mozambique, Tanzania
- Co-delivers five tailored investment-readiness programs supporting 150 sustainability-focused startups
- Current focus is sustainability/climate rather than pure fintech

**Note:** Village Capital has run 14+ accelerator programs in Africa since 2012. Fintech-specific programs have been in MENA/other regions, not currently in Malawi.

**Application:** https://vilcap.com/programs

### mHub (Malawi's Innovation Hub)

**Status:** Active -- Malawi's first technology and innovation hub
**Locations:** Lilongwe (main), Blantyre (working space)

- **Source:** https://www.connectingafrica.com/document.asp?doc_id=783094

### Key Observation
Malawi's fintech ecosystem is dominated by subsidiaries of larger African companies (Airtel, Mukuru, Mama Money) and telecom-backed mobile money (TNM, Airtel). Indigenous, venture-funded fintech startups are rare. This represents both a gap and an opportunity -- the ecosystem is underdeveloped compared to Kenya, Nigeria, or South Africa, meaning less competition for grant funding.

## 16. Regulatory Environment

### Current State (March 2026)

| Element | Status | Details |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Digital Bank Licensing | **NEW (2025)** | Directive 2025 enables digital-only banks |
| Mobile Money Regulation | **Established** | Airtel Money, TNM Mpamba licensed and regulated |
| E-Payment Mandate | **Active** | All licensed businesses must accept electronic payments |
| Regulatory Sandbox | **Not Yet** | No formal sandbox, but RBM collaborates with UNCDF on innovation |
| National Switch | **Operational** | MALSWITCH enables interoperability |
| NSFI III | **Active (2024-2028)** | Target 95% formal financial inclusion by 2028 |
| DFS Working Group | **Active (since 2022)** | Multi-stakeholder collaboration |
| Mobile Money Accounts | **9M+ registered** | As of 2022 (RBM data) |

### Regulatory Signals Favorable to Fintech
1. RBM co-leads the UNCDF Fintech Challenge -- actively seeking innovation
2. Digital Banking Directive 2025 signals openness to new entrants
3. Mandatory e-payment adoption creates market demand
4. NSFI III explicitly targets innovative financial service providers
5. DFS Working Group provides forum for regulatory engagement

## 17. Strategic Recommendations

### For a Malawian Fintech Business Seeking Grants

**Tier 1 -- Apply Immediately (Active, Malawi-specific)**
1. **Growth Accelerator Malawi** (mHub/GrowthAfrica) -- $10K-$40K, rolling rounds
2. **UNCDF Malawi Fintech Challenge** -- up to $100K, watch for new rounds
3. **Tony Elumelu Foundation** -- $5K seed + training, annual application (Jan-Mar)
4. **UNDP timbuktoo Fintech Accelerator** -- $25K, watch for new cohort calls

**Tier 2 -- Prepare Applications For (Periodic Windows)**
5. **ADFI (AfDB)** -- calls for proposals for digital financial inclusion
6. **Village Capital Africa programs** -- sustainability + innovation accelerators
7. **UNDP Public Sector Innovation Challenge** -- govtech solutions

**Tier 3 -- Position for Larger Opportunities**
8. **World Bank DMAP procurement** -- technology vendor contracts (requires company capability)
9. **IFAD FARMSE** -- rural fintech solutions (through government partnership)
10. **FSD Africa regional programs** -- financial inclusion innovation

**Tier 4 -- Long-Term Relationship Building**
11. **Engage with RBM DFS Working Group** -- regulatory visibility
12. **Join Africa Fintech Network** -- visibility to ADFI and pan-African opportunities
13. **Build on Mojaloop** (Gates Foundation open-source) -- for cross-border payments

### Key Success Factors
- **Matching funds:** Most grants (UNCDF, MICF, Growth Accelerator) require 30-50% co-funding
- **Impact metrics:** All funders want measurable impact on financial inclusion, women, youth, rural populations
- **Regulatory alignment:** Solutions aligned with NSFI III priorities have the best chance
- **Local registration:** Must be registered as for-profit in Malawi with a business license
- **Partnership approach:** Partner with established institutions (banks, MFIs, telecos) for credibility
- **Track record:** Most programs require 1-2 years of operations minimum

### Underserved Niches with Highest Grant Potential
Based on funder priorities, these solution areas attract the most grant funding in Malawi:
1. **VSLA digitization** -- transforming village savings groups with technology (UNCDF priority)
2. **Smallholder farmer finance** -- credit scoring using agricultural data (UNCDF, IFAD, USAID)
3. **Women's financial inclusion** -- products designed for women (FSD Africa, Gates Foundation, Mastercard Foundation)
4. **Cross-border SME payments** -- Malawi-Zambia-Tanzania-Mozambique corridors (Mojaloop, COMESA)
5. **Digital identity for financial access** -- linked to World Bank DMAP
6. **Agent banking / last-mile delivery** -- extending financial services to rural areas
