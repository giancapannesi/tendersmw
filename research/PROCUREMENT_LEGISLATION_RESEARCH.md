### 1.3 Key Changes from the 2017 Act (Cap. 37:03)

The 2017 Act (Act No. 27 of 2017) replaced the original Public Procurement Act of 2003 (Act No. 8 of 2003) and established the PPDA as an independent authority (previously the Office of the Director of Public Procurement, ODPP, under the 2003 Act).

The 2025 Act introduces these major reforms:

1. **ACB clearance for single-source procurement** (s.45(2)) -- A procuring entity must obtain clearance from the Anti-Corruption Bureau before signing any single-source procurement contract. This is entirely new.

2. **Beneficial ownership disclosure** (s.75(1)(c)) -- Bidders must disclose beneficial ownership in accordance with the Companies Act. New mandatory requirement.

3. **E-procurement mandate** (s.38(5), s.71) -- Procuring entities shall undertake procurement through the electronic government procurement system (MANEPS).

4. **PPDA levy collection power** (s.21) -- Authority may collect 1% levy on procurement/disposal contracts (less VAT). New self-funding mechanism.

5. **60% MSME reservation** (s.62(11)) -- 60% of national competitive bidding value must be reserved for Malawian MSMEs; 40% for others. Previously in policy/orders, now in the Act itself.

6. **Strengthened debarment** (s.79) -- Owners and directors of debarred companies are personally debarred (s.79(4)). Debarment decisions reviewable by High Court (s.79(5)).

7. **Unrealistically high bids** (s.64) -- Processing unrealistically high bids is now a criminal offence (fine K5,000,000 + 2 years imprisonment).

8. **Sustainable procurement** (s.30) -- New requirement for environmental protection and social/economic inclusion in procurement decisions.

9. **Reverse auction and forward auction** (s.48, s.52) -- New procurement/disposal methods for electronic systems.

10. **Strengthened penalties** -- Multiple new offence provisions with specific fines (K1M-K5M) and imprisonment terms.

11. **Procurement plan splitting prohibition** (s.55(2)-(3)) -- Splitting procurement to circumvent competitive methods is now a criminal offence (K5M fine + 2 years).

12. **Post-engagement restrictions** (s.80(1)(g), s.94) -- 2-year cooling-off period for former public officials before working on procurement contracts with former employers.

## 3. PPDA -- The Regulatory Authority

### 3.1 Establishment and Legal Mandate

The Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Authority (PPDA) is established under Section 4 of the PPDA Act 2025 as "a body corporate with perpetual succession and a common seal."

**Mandate (s.5(1)):** "The Authority shall be responsible for the regulation, monitoring and oversight of public procurement and disposal of public assets in Malawi."

**Independence (s.5(3)):** "The Authority shall perform its functions and exercise its powers provided in this Act in a manner that is impartial, and independent of the direction, influence or interference of any person, entity or authority."

### 3.2 Functions of the Authority (s.5(2))

- (a) Develop and enhance efficiency and effectiveness of public procurement
- (b) Develop standardized regulations, instructions, guidelines, and bidding documents binding on all PDEs
- (c) Collect data and monitor performance of PDEs and suppliers
- (d) Maintain and circulate lists of debarred bidders/suppliers/contractors
- (e) Provide annual procurement report to Minister (within 3 months of FY close) for tabling in National Assembly
- (f) Refer Act violations to budgetary and law enforcement authorities
- (g) Conduct administrative review of bid protests (Part XI)
- (h) Carry out economic studies and advise Government on procurement policy
- (i) Approve strategic plans, budgets and work plans
- (j) Facilitate capacity building of PDEs

### 3.3 Powers of the Authority (s.6)

Key powers include:

- **Set monetary thresholds** via Gazette notice (s.6(1)(a))
- **Issue "No Objection"** for procurements per threshold levels (s.6(1)(b)-(c))
- **Issue subpoenas** requiring production of procurement records (s.6(1)(d))
- **Access PDE premises** and request information (s.6(1)(e))
- **Summon and examine witnesses** (s.6(1)(f))
- **Suspend or debar** any bidder/supplier/contractor for Act breaches (s.6(1)(g))
- **Conduct procurement audits** at any time (s.6(1)(h))
- **Declare mis-procurement** and impose disciplinary/punitive measures (s.6(1)(i))
- **Investigate and sanction** PDEs or individuals involved in mis-procurement (s.6(1)(j))
- **Set conditions for MSME targeted procurement** (s.6(1)(k))
- **Issue MSME guidelines** (s.6(1)(m))
- **Develop e-procurement policies** (s.6(1)(l))

### 3.4 Governance Structure (s.7)

The Authority consists of nine members:

**Six appointed members (by Minister, subject to Public Appointments Committee approval):**
1. One person registered under the Malawi Institute of Procurement and Supply Act
2. One registered architect or quantity surveyor
3. One legal practitioner (valid practicing licence)
4. One member of Malawi Confederation of Chambers of Commerce and Industry
5. One engineer registered under the Malawi Engineering Institution Act
6. One member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in Malawi

**Three ex-officio members (or their designated representatives):**
1. Secretary to the President and Cabinet
2. Secretary to the Treasury
3. Solicitor General

- **Term:** 3 years, eligible for one re-appointment (s.8(1))
- **Quorum:** 5 members (s.9(5))
- **Gender consideration:** Minister must consider Gender Equality Act in appointments (s.7(4))
- **Meetings:** At least once every 3 months (s.9(1))

### 3.5 Director General (s.15-17)

- Appointed through open competitive process by the Authority
- Minimum: Master's degree in supply chain/finance/economics/engineering/accountancy/commerce/law/administration
- Minimum 10 years executive management experience
- Term: 3 years, one re-appointment possible
- Cannot hold a political office

## 4. Procurement Methods and Thresholds

### 4.1 Procurement Methods Under the 2025 Act

The 2025 Act establishes the following procurement methods in Part VII (Sections 38-54):

| Method | Section | When Used |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **National Competitive Bidding (NCB)** | s.40 | (a) Targets local enterprises; (b) Estimated value does not exceed amount prescribed by Authority; (c) Goods/works/services available competitively from >2 suppliers in Malawi; (d) No supplier identified via RFQ |
| **International Competitive Bidding (ICB)** | s.42 | (a) Estimated value exceeds amount prescribed by Authority; (b) Goods/works/services not available from >2 suppliers in Malawi; (c) No supplier identified via NCB |
| **Restricted Tendering** | s.41 | (a) Available from limited number of known suppliers; AND (b) Time/cost of considering large number of bids disproportionate to value |
| **Request for Proposals (RFP)** | s.43 | For procurement of consultancy services |
| **Request for Quotations (RFQ)** | s.44 | For goods, works and routine services when estimated value does not exceed amount prescribed by Authority |
| **Single Sourcing** | s.45 | (a) Value below prescribed threshold; (b) Only one supplier has capability/exclusive right; (c) Emergency; (d) Additional goods from same source for standardization/compatibility. **Requires ACB clearance before signing (s.45(2))** |
| **Two-Stage Tendering** | s.39 | When not feasible to define technical/contractual aspects fully at outset, or complex goods/works/services require exploring variants |
| **Force Account** | s.46 | Construction works using government's own resources/equipment/personnel (threshold for Authority approval applies) |
| **Community Participation** | s.47 | When community participation enhances economy/quality/sustainability, or objective is community employment |
| **Reverse Auction** | s.48 | Online real-time procurement for competitive pricing (requires e-procurement system) |
| **Emergency Procurement** | s.49 | May use RFQ, restricted tender, open tender, or single source. Post-procurement audit required. |
| **Procurement from Other PDEs** | s.50 | When competitive and brings value for money vs. other entities |
| **Framework Agreements** | s.54 | As prescribed in regulations; indefinite quantity at agreed unit price for agreed period |

### 4.2 Open Tender as Default (s.38)

Section 38(1): "A procuring and disposing entity shall undertake public procurement and disposal proceedings by means of **open methods of tender proceedings**, subject to the exceptions provided in this Act."

Any non-open method (except RFP for consultancy) requires **written application to the Authority** with sufficient reasons (s.38(2)-(4)).

### 4.3 Procurement Thresholds

**IMPORTANT:** The Act does NOT specify monetary threshold amounts. Instead, it delegates threshold-setting to the Authority via Gazette notice (s.6(1)(a)). Thresholds are published as PPDA circulars and adjusted periodically (most recently April 2024).

**Current thresholds (as of April 2024 PPDA Circular):**

The specific MK amounts were published in the "PPDA Circular -- Revision of Procurement Thresholds Effective 1st April 2024" available at [PPDA Circulars](https://ppda.mw/storage/documents/resources/PPDA%20CIRCULAR%20-%20REVISION%20OF%20PROCUREMENT%20THRESHOLDS%20EFFECTIVE%201ST%20APRIL%202024.pdf). The PDF is a scanned document (11MB) -- the exact amounts should be verified from the original.

**General threshold structure (from 2020 Regulations and subsequent circulars):**

| Procurement Method | Threshold Range (indicative) | Notes |
|--------------------|-----------------------------|-------|
| Request for Quotations | Up to a prescribed maximum | For low-value goods, works, routine services |
| National Competitive Bidding | Below ICB threshold | For procurement targeting local market |
| International Competitive Bidding | Above NCB threshold | For high-value or specialist procurement |
| Single Sourcing | Below a prescribed maximum | Plus ACB clearance requirement |
| PPDA No Objection | Above a prescribed threshold | Authority must review before award |
| Contract publication | Above MWK 50 million | Awards published on PPDA website |

**ACTION ITEM:** The exact current threshold amounts in MK should be obtained directly from PPDA or from the April 2024 circular PDF. These change periodically with inflation adjustments and are critical for accuracy.

### 4.4 Disposal Methods (s.51-52)

- **Default:** Open means -- public auction and sealed-bid tendering
- **Alternative** (with PPDA approval): Transfer to another PDE, destruction, trade-in, conversion, sale to employees (with restrictions), forward auction

## 5. Bidder Requirements

### 5.1 Eligibility Criteria (s.75)

A bidder must, to be eligible for a procurement contract:

1. **Be registered** with the Registrar of Companies or Registrar of Business Names, and with any applicable professional regulatory body (s.75(1)(a))
2. **Be tax compliant** (s.75(1)(b))
3. **Disclose beneficial ownership** in accordance with the Companies Act (s.75(1)(c))
4. **Not be debarred** from procurement under the Act (s.75(1)(d))

**Additional disqualifying conditions (s.75(2)):** A PDE shall not contract with a bidder that is:
- Bankrupt or insolvent
- In receivership
- Being wound up
- Under court/judicial administration
- Has suspended business activity
- Subject to legal proceedings

### 5.2 Qualification Criteria (s.76)

PDEs may assess bidders on:
- Legal capacity
- Professional and technical qualifications
- Financial resources and condition
- Equipment and other physical facilities
- Personnel and managerial capability
- Past performance (including legal disputes, convictions, false statements in last 3 years)

### 5.3 Supplier Registration with PPDA (s.77)

**Legal requirement:** Businesses are required by law to register with the PPDA to conduct business with Government Ministries, Departments and Agencies (MDAs).

**Process:**
- PPDA maintains a centralized supplier database (s.77(1))
- Registration within 30 working days of receipt of request (s.77(3))
- PDEs may maintain their own databases subject to PPDA approval (s.77(2))
- Processing time: 5 working days (if all documents in order)

**Required Documents:**
1. Business Registration Certificate or Certificate of Incorporation
2. MRA TPIN Certificate
3. General Receipt or Bank Deposit Slip (original, for registration fee payment)
4. MRA Tax Clearance Certificate (for renewals)
5. Previous PPDA Registration Certificate (for renewals)
6. Completed PPDA supplier registration form

### 5.4 Registration Fee Structure (GN 44/2025 and PPDA website)

**Domestic Suppliers (Goods, Services and Works):**

| Contract Value Threshold | Registration Fee (MK) |

**Foreign Suppliers:**

### 5.5 Service Categories for Registration

Suppliers register under specific categories including (non-exhaustive):
- Maintenance of motor vehicles

## 6. Procurement Process Rules

### 6.1 Publication of Invitations (s.56)

Invitations to tender or prequalify must be published in:
- (a) The Gazette
- (b) At least one widely circulated national newspaper
- (c) Electronic media
- (d) Websites of the PDE and PPDA
- (e) **For international tendering:** additionally in internationally recognized business portals and media of wide international circulation
- Plus displayed at the PDE's head office in a publicly accessible place (s.56(3))

### 6.2 Minimum Bidding Periods (s.58(5))

| Method | Minimum Period |
|--------|---------------|
| **International Competitive Bidding** | **30 days** |
| **National Competitive Bidding** | **20 days** |
| **Restricted Tender** | **15 days** |
| **Request for Proposals** | **15 days** |
| **Request for Quotations** | **5 days** |
| **Single Sourcing** | **5 days** |
| **Emergency Procurement** | **24 hours** |

A shorter period requires prior approval from the Authority (s.58(6)).

### 6.3 Standstill Period / Intention to Award (s.70)

**Section 70(1):** The PDE shall publish the notice of intention to award a contract in:
- The Gazette
- At least one widely circulated national newspaper
- The PPDA website
- The PDE's website

**For a period of fourteen (14) days** before signing the contract, for any procurement or disposal contract above a threshold set by the Authority.

The Authority may authorize a shorter publication period on application by the PDE (s.70(3)).

**Purpose:** This 14-day standstill period allows unsuccessful bidders to review the award decision and submit complaints/applications for review before the contract is signed.

### 6.4 Bid Security (s.68, s.59, s.62(10))

- **Bid security** is defined as "a security bond required for securing validity of a bid during the period stated in the bidding document" (s.2)
- A bid shall not be accepted if bid security was required and not provided (s.68(1)(c))
- Refusal to extend bid validity shall NOT result in forfeiture of bid security (s.59(3))
- Extension of bid validity requires extension of bid security (s.59(4))
- **Bid securing declaration** (alternative): an undertaking by a bidder committing to suspension from procurement if they violate stated conditions (s.68(2))
- Performance security may be required from the successful bidder before contract comes into force (s.62(10))

**NOTE:** Specific bid security percentage requirements (e.g., 1-5% of bid value) are prescribed in the Regulations and bidding documents, not in the Act itself.

### 6.5 Bid Opening (s.61)

- Bids opened at the time and place indicated in bidding documents
- Time of opening coincides with deadline for submission
- Bidders or representatives may attend (including virtually -- s.61(3))
- Names, addresses, and prices read out and recorded

### 6.6 Evaluation Criteria (s.62)

- Evaluation must follow criteria set in the bidding document (s.62(2))
- Evaluation must commence within 7 days of bid opening (s.62(2))
- **Lowest evaluated bid** wins for procurement (s.62(7)(a))
- **Highest evaluated bid** wins for disposal (s.62(7)(b))
- Margin of price preference may be applied for domestic/regional bidders per regulations (s.62(6))
- Non-price criteria must be objective and quantifiable where practicable (s.62(9))
- No negotiation on substance or price of bids (s.62(4)), except for single sourcing (s.69(3))

### 6.7 No Objection Requirement (s.6, s.72)

Contracts above prescribed thresholds require a "No Objection" from the Authority before the PDE can sign:
- Below a certain threshold: DG may issue No Objection
- Above that threshold: Authority (Board) must approve
- Contract preparation and signing only after No Objection (s.72(1))

### 6.8 Contract Management (s.73-74)

- Contract price generally fixed; modifications only if inflation changes significantly (s.73(1))
- Maximum contract extension period: 12 months (s.73(2)(a))
- Extensions beyond 12 months require PPDA No Objection (s.73(3))
- Price adjustments above contract limits require PPDA approval (s.73(2)(c))
- PDEs may terminate contracts for convenience in the public interest (s.74(4))

### 6.9 Record Keeping (s.32)

PDEs must maintain procurement/disposal records for **7 years** from:
- Contract completion or termination
- Decision to cancel proceedings
- Settlement of any dispute
- Resolution of any complaint/challenge/appeal

If not yet audited after 7 years, records must be maintained until audited.

### 6.10 Language (s.33)

All communication between PDEs and bidders/suppliers must be in **English** and **in writing**.

## 7. Complaints and Appeals

### 7.1 Right to Review (s.82)

A bidder that claims to have suffered, or may suffer, loss or injury due to breach of duty by the PDE or Authority may seek a review under Part XI.

**Grounds that do NOT constitute basis for review (s.82(2)):**
- Choice of selection procedure in RFP for services
- Decision to reject all bids under s.66
- Refusal to respond to expression of interest in RFP for consultancy

### 7.2 Review Procedure (s.83)

**Step 1 -- Application to Controlling Officer:**
- Must be submitted during the 14-day intention-to-award period; OR
- Within 14 days of the bidder becoming aware of the circumstances (or when they should have become aware), whichever is earlier (s.83(1))
- Application in writing to the Controlling Officer (s.83(2))
- Controlling Officer must render a decision within **14 days** (s.83(9))

**Step 2 -- Appeal to PPDA:**
- If not satisfied with the Controlling Officer's response
- Written application to the Authority (s.83(3))
- Must be accompanied by prescribed fees (s.83(4))
- Authority convenes a three-member technical committee to hear the application and make recommendations (s.83(6))

**Suspension of proceedings:**
- Upon receipt of application for review, procurement/disposal proceedings are **suspended for 10 days** (s.83(7))
- May be extended to **30 days** if dispute not resolved (s.83(8))

**PPDA remedial powers (s.83(10)):**
- (a) Prohibit PDE from acting unlawfully
- (b) Annul unlawful act or decision (whole or part)
- (c) Revise unlawful decision or substitute its own decision
- (d) Compel PDE to pay applicant's costs of bid preparation and review
- (e) Compel applicant to pay costs of the review
- (f) Report the public official to oversight bodies for disciplinary action and prosecution

**Step 3 -- Judicial Review:**
- Any PPDA decision is subject to review by the **High Court** (s.83(11))

### 7.3 Debarment Process (s.79)

**Grounds for debarment (s.79(2)):**
- (a) Supplying false information in bids/prequalification
- (b) Collusion between bidders or bidder and public official concerning bidding documents
- (c) Interference with competing bidders' participation
- (d) Misconduct: corruption, price fixing, under-pricing pattern, confidentiality breach, contract execution misconduct
- (e) Conviction for offence relating to obtaining procurement contract
- (f) Conviction for dishonesty or fraud in professional activity
- (g) Submitting misleading bid information
- (h) Refusing to accept award within validity period (where no bid security required)
- (i) Repeated failure to comply with contract terms
- (j) Material breach of contract
- (k) Unauthorized assignment or subcontracting

**Debarment features:**
- Period commensurate with gravity of misconduct (s.79(3))
- **Owners and directors** of debarred entities are **personally debarred** (s.79(4))
- Right of hearing before debarment decision (s.79(3) -- "found liable after the hearing")
- Aggrieved party may apply to **High Court for judicial review** (s.79(5))
- PPDA maintains and circulates debarment lists (s.5(2)(d))

### 7.4 Recent Debarment Actions (October 2025)

Five companies debarred in October 2025:

- **NTK Investments** (Blantyre) -- 48 months (contravened s.79(2)(h) -- fraudulent/unethical conduct)

## 8. Anti-Corruption Provisions

### 8.1 ACB Clearance for Single-Source Procurement (s.45(2)-(4))

**NEW in 2025 Act:** "A procuring and disposing entity shall obtain clearance issued by the Anti-Corruption Bureau pursuant to the powers conferred on the Bureau under section 10 of the Corrupt Practices Act, before a single sourcing procurement contract is signed." (s.45(2))

- ACB clearance request can be submitted concurrently with the PPDA submission (s.45(3))
- Authority shall prescribe a threshold for single-sourcing requiring ACB clearance (s.45(4))
- This provision addresses a major corruption vulnerability -- procurement irregularities constitute over 70% of all corruption cases handled by the ACB

### 8.2 Corrupt Practices Act (Cap. 7:04)

- **Original enactment:** December 1995
- **Amended:** 2004 (Act No. 17 of 2004)
- **Establishes:** Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB)
- **Section 10:** Powers of the ACB to investigate and prevent corruption, including vetting procurement decisions
- **Definition:** "Corrupt practice" in the PPDA Act has the meaning from the Corrupt Practices Act (s.2)

### 8.3 Conduct of Bidders and Suppliers (s.78)

Bidders and suppliers shall not engage in or abet:
- (a) Corrupt practices (offering improper inducements)
- (b) Fraudulent practices (misrepresentation of facts)
- (c) Collusion among bidders (artificial, non-competitive price levels)

Any malpractice must be reported immediately to the PDE head, PPDA, and law enforcement authorities (s.78(2)).

### 8.4 Conduct of Public Officials (s.80)

Public officials involved in procurement must:
- Discharge duties impartially (s.80(1)(a))
- Act in the public interest (s.80(1)(b))
- Avoid conflicts of interest (s.80(1)(c))
- Not commit or abet corrupt/fraudulent practices (s.80(1)(d))
- Not interfere with or exert undue influence (s.80(1)(e))
- Keep information confidential (s.80(1)(f))
- **2-year cooling-off period** after leaving public service before involvement in private sector procurement with former employer (s.80(1)(g))

### 8.5 Disclosure of Interest (s.93)

- Members of the Authority, committees, IPDCs, evaluation teams, and procurement units must disclose any direct or indirect interest in matters before them
- Must be disclosed before or at the start of meetings
- Person with interest cannot participate in discussion or vote
- If not disclosed and decision benefits that person/relative/business partner: **decision is null and void** (s.93(3), s.93(6))

### 8.6 Penalties (s.96 and specific sections)

## 9. Special Provisions -- Local Preference and MSME

### 9.1 MSME Participation (s.37)

**Section 37(1):** A PDE may:
- (a) **Grant a margin of preference** for bids by MSMEs offering goods, works, consultancy or services manufactured, grown or performed by a local enterprise
- (b) **Reserve or set aside** certain procurement requirements for MSMEs by restricting bidding to those enterprises

**Section 37(2):** The Authority, in consultation with the Minister responsible for MSMEs, may prescribe preferences or reservations for MSMEs by Gazette notice.

### 9.2 60% MSME Reservation (s.62(11))

**Section 62(11):** "A procuring and disposing entity shall ensure that **sixty per cent (60%)** of the value of procurements carried out in a financial year under national competitive bidding is reserved for Malawian micro, small and medium sized enterprises and **forty per cent (40%)** is reserved for other bidders."

This 60/40 split is now enshrined in the primary legislation (previously in subsidiary orders/circulars).

### 9.3 Margin of Price Preference (s.62(6))

**Section 62(6):** "In the evaluation of bids, a procuring and disposing entity may apply a margin of price preference in favour of domestic or regional bidders in accordance with the regulations, provided that such application has been disclosed in the bidding documents."

**In practice:** PPDA has issued a circular for a 20% margin of preference for indigenous Malawian bidders when competing with foreign businesses, meaning local companies can be awarded contracts even if their bid is up to 20% higher than the foreign bidder's.

### 9.4 MSME Order 2020 (GN 96/2020)

The MSME Order prescribes:
- Preferences and reservations for MSMEs
- Reserved procurement for marginalized groups: **youth, women, and persons with disabilities (PWDs)**
- Set-aside procurement categories restricted to MSME bidding

**Definition of "local enterprise" (s.2 of the Act):** "supplier, contractor or consulting firm registered and principally operating in Malawi and whose shareholding is held in majority by Malawian nationals"

**Definition of MSMEs (s.2):** "enterprises, including their affiliates, that are independently owned and operated, not dominant in the field of operation in which they are bidding on Government contracts and qualified as such under the Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Act, 2024" (Act No. 30 of 2024)

### 9.5 "Domestic bidder" and "Regional bidder" Definitions (s.2)

- **Domestic bidder:** any bidder whose principal place of business is within Malawi
- **Regional bidder:** a bidder from countries in the region or regional block as prescribed in the regulations

### 9.6 Sustainable Procurement (s.30)

PDEs must consider:
- Environmental protection
- Social and economic inclusion
- Procurement that stimulates innovation

## 10. International and Donor Procurement Rules

### 10.1 Donor Procurement Exemption (s.3(2)(j))

**Section 3(2)(j):** The Act does NOT apply to "procurement under donor-financed projects if:
- (i) this Act is in conflict with the procurement rules of the donor or the financing agency; and
- (ii) the application of the donor or the financing agency rule is mandatory in the financing agreement entered into by the Government and the donor or the financing agency."

**Key principle:** Where donor rules conflict with national law AND the financing agreement makes donor rules mandatory, donor rules prevail. Otherwise, national procurement law applies.

### 10.2 World Bank Procurement Framework

The World Bank uses its 2016 Procurement Framework for all projects approved after 1 July 2016:

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Framework** | World Bank Procurement Regulations for IPF Borrowers (2016, revised) |
| **System** | STEP (Systematic Tracking of Exchanges in Procurement) |
| **Methods** | Open Competitive Bidding (OCB) preferred. National/International competitive methods. |
| **National procedures** | May be used when approaching national market per paragraph 5.3 of Procurement Regulations |
| **International procedures** | Bank's Standard Procurement Documents (SPDs) must be used |
| **Key principle** | Open international competitive procurement with wide advertising for high-value/high-risk contracts |
| **Eligibility** | Firms from all Bank member countries eligible (with exceptions for sanctions) |
| **Thresholds** | Project-specific, defined in each project's Procurement Plan in STEP |
| **Recent Malawi projects** | Malawi Governance to Enable Service Delivery Project; Digital Malawi Acceleration Project; Malawi Watershed Services Improvement Project |
| **World Bank guidance** | [Procurement Framework](https://www.worldbank.org/ext/en/what-we-do/project-procurement/framework) |

### 10.3 European Union / EDF Procurement

### 10.6 Key Principle for Donor Projects

When a tender opportunity is funded by an international donor, bidders should check:
1. **Who is financing?** -- determines which procurement rules apply
2. **Does the financing agreement mandate donor rules?** -- if yes, donor rules override national law
3. **What are the specific thresholds?** -- these vary by project and donor
4. **Where is it advertised?** -- donor-funded projects often require publication on specific portals (STEP, TED, UNGM, dgMarket)
