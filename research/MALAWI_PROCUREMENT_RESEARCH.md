## 3. Number of Tenders Per Year

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Contracts awarded (2022)** | ~1,872 | From aggregator data |
| **Estimated tenders published annually** | ~2,000-3,000 | Including cancelled/re-tendered |
| **Tenders on BidDetail (snapshot)** | ~1,000 live | At any given time |
| **PPDA published notices** | Not fully digitized | Only above MWK 50M disclosed |
| **Newspaper-only tenders** | 10-30 per day | From Nation/Daily Times print editions |
| **Estimated newspaper tenders/year** | ~3,000-7,000 | 250 working days x 10-30/day |
| **MANEPS tenders (pilot)** | 32 PDEs onboarded | As of early 2026 |
| **MANEPS mandatory from** | April 1, 2026 | All PDEs must use system |

**Key insight:** The actual number of procurement activities is likely MUCH higher than 1,872 because:
1. Many tenders below MWK 50M are not published on PPDA website
2. Newspaper-only tenders (estimated 3,000-7,000/year) are not digitized
3. Request for Quotations (RFQ) for smaller purchases are not publicly advertised
4. Donor-funded procurement often uses parallel systems

**Realistic estimate:** 5,000-10,000+ procurement activities per year across all methods and channels.

**Comparison -- Tanzania:** Tanzania's NeST e-procurement system had 77,595 planned tenders as of April 2024, with 1,147 registered procuring entities and ~20,800 contracts awarded since July 2023. ([PPRA Tanzania](https://www.ppra.go.tz/publications/annual-procurement-reports)) Malawi, at roughly 1/6th Tanzania's GDP, would proportionally expect 3,500-13,000 tenders/year.

## 6. Procurement Thresholds

### PPDA Circular -- Thresholds Effective July 1, 2023

Source: [PPDA Circular](https://ppda.mw/storage/documents/resources/PPDA%20CIRCULAR%20-%20THRESHOLDS%20EFFECTIVE%201ST%20JULY%202023.PDF)

The PDF was not extractable remotely but the PPDA system uses these procurement methods with thresholds:

| Method | Application | Estimated Threshold (MWK) |
|--------|------------|--------------------------|
| **Open Competitive Bidding (OCB)** | Default method for all procurement above threshold | Above ~MWK 200M for goods, higher for works |
| **Restricted Bidding** | Limited competition, pre-qualified suppliers | Below OCB threshold, above RFQ |
| **Request for Quotations (RFQ)** | Small value procurement | Below ~MWK 50-100M |
| **Single Source / Direct** | Emergency or sole supplier | Any value with justification |

Source: [PPDA Supplier Registration](https://www.ppda.mw/supplier-registration)

### Disclosure Threshold

PPDA publishes contract award information only for awards **above MWK 50 million** (~$29,000). Below this threshold, awards are not publicly disclosed on the PPDA website. ([PPDA Award Notices](https://ppda.mw/award-notices))

### New Act (2025)

The Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Act No. 7 of 2025 came into force March 31, 2025. Key changes:
- Mandatory beneficial ownership disclosure
- Stricter pricing controls
- Simplified procedures
- Clearer definitions of procurement roles

Source: [PPDA Act 2025](https://ppda.mw/storage/documents/resources/PUBLIC%20PROCUREMENT%20AND%20DISPOSAL%20OF%20PUBLIC%20ASSETS%20ACT,%202025.pdf), [PPDA News](https://www.ppda.mw/newsdetail/29)

| **Supplier categories** | 7 categories by value | See Section 6 above |

### Market Structure

- **Dominated by few large, well-connected firms.** A narrow set of larger firms dominates the public procurement market. Smaller local firms struggle to participate. ([World Bank](https://thedocs.worldbank.org/en/doc/529a0a0fdcb474864fdbd14c7af8c0f9-0010012025/original/MEM-21-01-4-web.pdf))
- **Complex registration process** deters SME participation
- **Widespread use of restricted tendering** has undermined competition and created procurement monopolies
- **International firms** compete mainly on donor-funded projects (World Bank, AfDB, EU procurement follows separate rules)
- **Chinese, Indian, and South African firms** dominate large infrastructure works

### Comparison: Zambia

Zambia's ZPPA registered 3,808 suppliers as of 2022. ([ZPPA 2022 Annual Report](https://www.zppa.org.zm/documents/20182/0/ZPPA++2022+Annual+Report.pdf)) Malawi likely has a similar or slightly lower number given its smaller economy.

## 8. Success Rates & Competition

**Hard data on bid success rates in Malawi is not publicly available.** This is itself a transparency gap. However, based on available evidence:

### Estimated Competition Levels

| Factor | Estimate | Basis |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Average bidders per open tender** | 3-7 bidders | Regional benchmarks + market structure |
| **Average bidders per restricted tender** | 2-3 bidders | By definition, restricted |
| **Bid success rate (open)** | ~15-30% | 1/3 to 1/7 success rate |
| **Single source prevalence** | Reportedly high | [HIVOS Brief](https://hivos.org/assets/2021/02/Malawi-Brief-on-Public-Procurement.pdf) |
| **Repeat winners** | Common | Market dominated by few firms |

### Structural Issues

- Restricted tendering is **overused**, reducing competitive pressure
- Public officials use **exclusionary procurement methods** (accepting bids from a single source) to prevent competition ([Open Government Partnership](https://www.opengovpartnership.org/malawi-open-contracting-story/))
- **Legal loopholes** previously allowed businesses to bid without disclosing beneficial owners (addressed in 2025 Act)
- Competition is **better on donor-funded tenders** which follow World Bank/AfDB procurement rules

## 9. Common Problems & Challenges

### Documented Issues

| Problem | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **Corruption perception** | 66% of Malawians said corruption increased "a lot" or "somewhat" in the past year (2022) | [OGP](https://www.opengovpartnership.org/malawi-open-contracting-story/) |
| **Late/non-payment to suppliers** | Government arrears totaling ~$60 million; suppliers demand upfront payment | [Nation Online](https://mwnation.com/fuel-suppliers-wanted-payment-first-minister/) |
| **Procurement monopolies** | Narrow set of large firms dominates; restricted tendering undermines competition | [World Bank](https://thedocs.worldbank.org/en/doc/529a0a0fdcb474864fdbd14c7af8c0f9-0010012025/original/MEM-21-01-4-web.pdf) |
| **Manual processes** | Long delays and vulnerability to corruption due to paper-based systems | [OGP](https://www.opengovpartnership.org/malawi-open-contracting-story/) |
| **Beneficial ownership opacity** | Previously no requirement to disclose who actually owns bidding companies | [OGP](https://www.opengovpartnership.org/malawi-open-contracting-story/) |
| **SME exclusion** | Complex registration, capacity constraints, and perceived corruption deter small businesses | [MAPS Assessment](https://www.mapsinitiative.org/en/countries/malawi.html) |
| **Capacity constraints at PDEs** | Many procuring entities lack trained procurement officers | [PPDA](https://ppda.mw/) |
| **Austerity non-compliance** | Government entities repeatedly fail to follow austerity procurement orders | [CIJM](https://www.investigative-malawi.org/3072/malawi-austerity-orders/) |
| **Patronage in budget** | Budget allocation influenced by political patronage rather than need | [CIJM](https://www.investigative-malawi.org/2979/malawi-budget-2026-2027/) |

### Reform Efforts Underway

1. **MANEPS e-procurement** -- Mandatory from April 2026; 32 pilot PDEs already onboarded
2. **New PPDA Act (2025)** -- Beneficial ownership disclosure, stricter pricing, simpler procedures
3. **Open Contracting Data Standard (OCDS)** -- Publishing procurement data in machine-readable format
4. **World Bank Digital Malawi Project** -- Funding the e-GP platform and e-Marketplace for MSMEs
5. **Open Government Partnership** -- Malawi is a member, committed to procurement transparency

Sources: [PPDA Act 2025](https://ppda.mw/storage/documents/resources/PUBLIC%20PROCUREMENT%20AND%20DISPOSAL%20OF%20PUBLIC%20ASSETS%20ACT,%202025.pdf), [PPDA MANEPS](https://ppda.mw/newsdetail/30), [OGP Malawi](https://www.opengovpartnership.org/malawi-open-contracting-story/)

### Digitization Trajectory

| Milestone | Date | Impact |
|-----------|------|--------|
| e-GP concept introduced | 2020 | Planning phase |
| MANEPS developed (Perago + Mitra Systems) | 2023-2024 | System built |

### Key Growth Drivers

1. **MANEPS going mandatory** -- All ~200 PDEs must digitize procurement from April 2026. This will dramatically increase the number of publicly visible tenders.
2. **World Bank portfolio expanding** -- $3.23B active portfolio with new mega-projects (Mpatamanga alone = $1.5B)
3. **EU Global Gateway** -- EUR 1.17B in investments flowing through Malawi
4. **Infrastructure push** -- Transport budget grew 64% in 2024/25
5. **AfDB strategy 2023-2028** -- Prioritizes energy, transport, water, agriculture
6. **Open contracting push** -- More data becoming publicly accessible

### Key Differences

1. **Tanzania is far ahead on e-procurement** -- NeST has 77,595 planned tenders and 20,800 contracts. Malawi's MANEPS has 32 pilot PDEs. Tanzania shows what Malawi could look like in 2-3 years.
2. **Zambia has a more mature regulatory system** -- ZPPA has published annual reports since 2015 with detailed statistics.
3. **Malawi's ODA dependency is the highest** -- 73% of development budget from donors vs. ~40-50% for neighbors. This means donor-funded procurement rules (World Bank, AfDB, EU) apply to a larger share of Malawi's procurement.
4. **Malawi is the smallest economy** -- But this also means less competition in the digital tender aggregation space.

### Implications for TendersMW

1. **Market size is real** -- $1.2-2.1B annual procurement is a meaningful market even at modest monetization rates.
2. **MANEPS timing is perfect** -- Mandatory digital procurement from April 2026 creates a massive new data source right as we launch.
3. **Newspaper moat is valuable** -- 3,000-7,000 newspaper-only tenders/year are completely undigitized. Whoever captures these has unique data.
4. **Donor-funded procurement is huge** -- $1.7B ODA with 73% of dev budget from donors. International bidders need a platform to find Malawi opportunities.
5. **Competition is weak** -- Indian aggregators have generic platforms. No dedicated, modern, local Malawi platform exists.
6. **SME pain point is real** -- Complex registration, procurement monopolies, and corruption mean SMEs need help navigating the system. Content + alerts = value.
7. **Growth trajectory is steep** -- Budget growing 24-39% YoY, digitization accelerating, more data becoming available every quarter.
